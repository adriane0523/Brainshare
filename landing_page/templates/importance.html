Importance of BRAINshare

Project Narrative Project Aims Modified Policy Delphi Importance

The BRAIN Initiative promises to significantly advance neuroscience with the development of novel neurotechnologies. To promote equity and maximize the impact of the investment in these research projects, BRAIN Initiative studies are required to provide a plan for sharing data and materials. Further, developing standards to facilitate data sharing, while considering—in a “serious and sustained manner”— ethical issues, such as data privacy and the appropriate use of brain data, and establishing data-sharing platforms, like the Data Archive BRAIN Initiative (DABI) for invasive neurophysiology data, are identified as core principles for the future of the BRAIN Initiative in the Working Group Report to the Advisory Committee to the Director of NIH (ACD), “BRAIN 2025.” In its 2019 Neuroethics Roadmap,1 the BRAIN 2.0 Neuroethics Subgroup (BNS) also identified as one of five key priorities for the BRAIN Initiative the need to develop data- sharing standards that will advance science while respecting individual privacy. BRAIN Initiative investigators (including mPI Dr. Sheth) who are funded through the Investigative Human Neuroscience Funding Opportunity Announcements collaborate in an NIH-coordinated Research Opportunities in Humans (ROH) Consortium work group, which aims “to identify consensus standards of practice, including neuroethical considerations, to collect and provide data for ancillary studies, and to aggregate and standardize data for dissemination among the wider scientific community.” The proposed project will support the work of the ROH Consortium work group, and more broadly the development of formal policies and guidelines within the BRAIN Initiative, by providing critical empirical data about specific data sharing challenges and concerns and generating policy and practice options through an extensive process of deliberation involving multiple diverse stakeholder groups.
